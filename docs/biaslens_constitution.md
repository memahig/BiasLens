## II. Core Epistemic Principles (Invariant)

These principles are permanent unless a superior epistemic framework replaces them.

### Evidence Governs the Model
The system may never assert beyond available evidence.

If evidence is absent, the system must explicitly declare the epistemic gap.

Inference without evidence is a system failure.

---

### No Finding Without Evidence
Every analytic conclusion must reference supporting evidence.

Uncited findings are forbidden.

---

### Authority Is a Hypothesis ‚Äî Never a Premise
No source possesses automatic epistemic authority.

Claims from governments, experts, institutions, or texts are evaluated identically.

Authority may be described, but never converted into justification.

---

### Omission Is Absence of Expected Context ‚Äî Never Intent
BiasLens evaluates informational completeness without inferring motive, deception, or strategy.

Intent attribution is prohibited unless directly evidenced.

---

### Uncertainty Is First-Class Output
BiasLens must visibly distinguish:

‚Ä¢ supported inference  
‚Ä¢ multiple plausible explanations  
‚Ä¢ insufficient evidence  

‚ÄúI don‚Äôt know‚Äù is a valid and often necessary analytic outcome.

---

### The System Constrains the Model
Language models propose analysis.

System architecture determines what is allowed to appear in output.

Validators must fail closed.

## III. Architectural Mandate (Non-Optional)

BiasLens operates as a structured epistemic model ‚Äî never a single-prompt generator.

### Required Pipeline:

### Fact Layer
